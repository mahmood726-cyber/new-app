💡 CI Method Recommendation

HKSJ (Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman)

Recommended for most meta-analyses.

  • Uses t-distribution instead of normal distribution
  • Produces wider, more accurate confidence intervals
  • Better coverage probability, especially with few studies (k < 10)
  • More conservative when heterogeneity is uncertain

Reference: IntHout J, et al. BMC Med Res Methodol. 2014;14:25

Wald (Standard)

Traditional method, may be preferred for comparability.

  • Uses normal (z) distribution for confidence intervals
  • Standard in older software and publications
  • May have poor coverage with few studies or high heterogeneity
  • Use when comparing with published meta-analyses using Wald CIs

Reference: Borenstein M, et al. Introduction to Meta-Analysis. Wiley, 2009

Quick Start Guide

        

            

                
1. Search for Studies

                
Use the PICO form to search clinical trial registries. Enter your Population, Intervention, Comparator, and Outcomes.

            

            

                
2. Extract Data

                
Upload PDF files or enter data manually. The platform extracts effect sizes, sample sizes, and other relevant data.

            

            

                
3. Run Analysis

                
Select your effect measure and model type. Click "Run Analysis" to compute pooled estimates with forest and funnel plots.

            

            

                
4. Export Results

                
Export to CSV, JSON, or generate reproducible R code. Download PRISMA flow diagrams and validation reports.
